Title: Hwachae (Korean Fruit Punch)

Description:

Hwachae is a traditional fruit punch from Korean, dating back to 57 BC - 668 AD. Modern day hwachae is often made with strawberry milk and some kind of carbonated beverage, such as sprite or cider, and filled with chopped up fruits. It is also common for hwachae to be served inside a watermelon. Recently, hwachae has been going viral on the internet, praised for being a refreshing and hydrating treat.

Ingredients:

Some common ingredients in hwachae are watermelon, milk/strawberry milk, berries, mangoes, banana, honeydew, kiwi, jelly, sprite, and ice cubes. Hwachae is easily customizable to what you have in your pantry, so most fruits and refreshing drinks will work.

Instructions:

  1. Chop up your watermelon and fruits into bite sized cubes.
  2. Place watermelon pieces (and the juice) in a large mixing bowl.
  3. Mix the milk and carbonated beverage of choice in another bowl and pour the mixture over the watermelon. Garnish with berries/any other additional fruit if you are using.
  4. To serve, ladle the punch into individual serving bowls. Serve immediately or chill for up to 2 hours.
  5. Leftover punch can be stored in the fridge for up to 24 hours.
